Abstract

"Replicating reference camera trajectories unlocks immense potential for cinematic video creation. However, standard Diffusion Transformers (DiTs) face quadratic token complexity and slow iterative sampling..."

We present FastCamClone, a framework that decouples conditioning mechanisms to eliminate overhead. We inject camera motion as macroscopic signals directly into noise latents (SPMI) and resolve content-target ambiguity via Phase-shifted RoPE. Our Preference Optimization Distillation (POD) enables high-fidelity, few-step generation by treating reference latents as positive anchors.

Methodology

Decoupled Conditional Injection

Splits content and camera cues. Uses Single-Point Macro Injection (SPMI) to slash attention complexity from \(9N^2\) to \(4N^2\), accelerating inference by 2×.

Preference Optimization Distillation (POD)

An RL-based distillation scheme that anchors student predictions to both teacher steps and ground-truth targets, enabling cinematic results in just 4-8 steps.
